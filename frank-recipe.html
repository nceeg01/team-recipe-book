Mofongo (Puerto Rican Garlic Mashed Plantains)

By: Frank Mucheke

Ingredients:

  • 3 green plantains
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 tablespoons olive oil (or pork fat)
  • 1/2 cup chicharrón (fried pork rinds) or cooked bacon bits
  • Salt, to taste
  • Pepper, to taste
  • Optional: chicken broth for moistening

Instructions:

  1. Peel the green plantains and cut them into 1-inch slices.
  2. In a deep skillet, heat oil over medium heat and fry the plantains until golden brown (about 5–7 minutes per side). Remove and drain on paper towels.
  3. In a mortar and pestle (or bowl), mash the garlic with a bit of olive oil and salt until a paste forms.
  4. Add the fried plantains and chicharrón to the mortar and mash together until well combined. Add a splash of chicken broth if the mixture is too dry.
  5. Shape the mofongo into a round mound or serve it in a small bowl.
  6. Serve hot with a side of broth, shrimp, chicken, or your favorite meat.

Cooking time: 30 minutes

Serves: 3–4 people
